For All the Dogs by Drake

On Wednesday September 6, Drake revealed the official release date for his newest studio album, For All the Dogs. This is the confirmation fans have been waiting for surrounding the speculation of a rumoured eighth studio album. Known features include latin artist Bad Bunny and longtime collaborator with Drake, Nicki Minaj. The album features production from on the rise producer BNYX and creative direction from Lil Yachty.

The Meaning Behind the Cover Art

Drake revealed the cover art before announcing the official release date of the album. The album art consists of a white dog with red eyes drawn in crayon on top of a black background. The art was actually drawn by his 5-year-old son Adonis Graham. Drake has notoriously received mixed reviews for his controversial album art for his past three albums. Although the art received mixed reviews his son received a lumpsome of money from his father for the work.

How Will This Compare to Drake's Catalogue?

In the past decade, Drake has managed to stay afloat, releasing hit singles and impactful albums. How will For All the Dogs stack up against his other hit records? Let's analyze. We'll start with his first week album sales.

  1. Views (2016), 1,040,000
  2. Scorpion (2018), 732,000
  3. Nothing Was the Same (2013), 658,000

Now let's look at Spotify daily streams of his top albums.

It's unlikely Drake will beat his own debut week of 1,040,000 records sold. Yet, he does have the third most No. 1 albums on the Billboard 200 charts, maybe he'll add another. He could also surpass his daily Spotify streams with this new album. Only time will tell.
